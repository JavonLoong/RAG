Church of All Saints is a Grade I listed church in Cople, Bedfordshire, England.
 It became a listed building on 13 July 1964.
 The church, dedicated to All Saints, is constructed of sandstone and Dunstable clunch, in the Early English Period and Perpendicular styles.
 It contains a chancel with side chapels, nave with clerestory, aisles, south porch and a tower containing 5 bells.
 On the north side of the chancel, there is a marble altar tomb.
 The nave and chancel have been restored since 1877.
 The register dates from the year 1560.
 Its patron was Christ Church, Oxford.
